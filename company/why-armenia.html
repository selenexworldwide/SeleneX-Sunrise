Our Culture

A Culture of
Connection & Mentorship

In Armenia, as across Asia and Eastern Europe, the relationship between seniors and juniors is deeply rooted in mutual respect—and this is what makes us different.

Seniors

Experienced professionals are treated with high regard. Their expertise is valued, their guidance actively sought. Decades of hard-won wisdom aren't just acknowledged—they're revered.

Juniors

Junior talent brings remarkable capabilities—fresh perspectives, cutting-edge skills, and boundless energy. But they understand that their seniors carry experience that only time can provide.

The Connection

Knowledge flows freely between generations. Innovation happens not in silos, but through collaboration between visionary juniors and battle-tested seniors.

This is why Armenia's tech scene thrives

We are connected. The hierarchy isn't about power—it's about growth. Seniors mentor because they care. Juniors listen because they're wise enough to learn. This creates teams that don't just build products—they build legacies.

Fun Facts About Armenia

A land of ancient history, breathtaking landscapes, and surprising connections to world heritage. Armenia is one of the oldest continuously inhabited regions in the world.

Did you know? Cradle of Civilizations

The term "Caucasian," used by Americans and Europeans to identify their ancestry, originates here. Armenia is also the only modern country on the oldest known world map (Babylonian, 6th c. BC).

Origin: Armenian Highlands Historic Hub
3,000 Years

Ancient Capital

Yerevan was founded in 782 BC—29 years before Rome. It's one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities.

Heritage

Land of Noah

Tradition holds Armenians as descendants of Noah's great-great-grandson Hayk. The country is called "Hayastan".

5,137m

Mt. Ararat

Noah's Ark is said to have landed on Mt. Ararat. It remains the sacred national symbol of Armenia.

301 AD

First Christian State

In 301 AD, Armenia became the first nation to adopt Christianity as its state religion, predating Rome.

6,100 Years

World's Oldest Winery

The Areni-1 cave complex contains the world's oldest known winery. Armenia is a birthplace of viticulture.

405 AD

Unique Alphabet

Invented in 405 AD by Mesrop Mashtots with 36 original letters (now 39). It has been the backbone of Armenian culture for 1,600+ years.

Apricot

National Symbol

The scientific name "Prunus armeniaca" means Armenian plum. The apricot's orange color is on the national flag.
