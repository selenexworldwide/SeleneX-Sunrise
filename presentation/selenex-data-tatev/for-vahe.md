### 1. Overall Idea

We’re telling a **coherent product story** about **SeleneX** — a **GenAI super-agent and Synthetic Data Generation Engine (SDGE)** for **early and equitable ovarian cancer care**, especially in **low-resource settings** and in collaboration with **UNICEF** and global partners.

The flow should feel like:

> Problem → Solution → Proof it’s real (engine, architecture, code, prototype) → People & partners → Business model & sustainability.
>

### 2. Number of Slides & Structure

- **Total: 9 main slides**
- **Slide 8** has **sub-slides/sections** for team & partners
    
    (Block A: core team, Block B: clinical/scientific experts, Block C: institutional partners).
    

**Slide map:**

1. **Intro** – What SeleneX is + key keywords + partner logos
2. **Problem** – Why women are diagnosed too late
3. **Solution** – SeleneX: early detection, personalization, continuous follow-up
4. **What We Built** – Synthetic Data Generation Engine (SDGE) & curated data library
5. **Architecture** – SDGE pipeline (modules 1, 2, 3A, 3B, evaluation layer)
6. **Codebase Overview** – GitHub repo + modular structure
7. **Prototype Walkthrough** – SDGE UI: upload → configure → generate → audit → export
8. **Team & Partnerships**
    - 8A: Overview of Blocks A/B/C
    - 8B: Block A – core SeleneX SDG team (founders, engineers, product/UX)
    - 8C: Block B – clinical & scientific expert council (oncology, radiology, trials, etc.)
    - 8D: Block C – institutional & governance partners (Insight Arcs, IESE, D-Clinic, ANDglobal, UT, etc.)
9. **Business Model** – Open-core strategy, revenue streams, equity principle

### 3. Theme & Visual Direction

- **Theme:**
    - *“Serious, global healthtech + AI, with a strong equity and women’s health focus.”*
    - It should feel **clinical, trustworthy, and modern**, not “startup-hype.”
- **Visual style:**
    - Clean, minimal slides with lots of white/very light backgrounds.
    - Accent colors: **deep blue / teal / UNICEF-compatible tones**.
    - Consistent **device mockup** (iPad/iPod-like) wherever we show the product, code, or prototype (Slides 4, 6, 7).
    - Use **real logos** (media, universities, partners) and **real headshots** (from Notion) to prove credibility.
- **Narrative tone:**
    - Female-led, global, clinically grounded.
    - Emphasize **early detection**, **synthetic data**, **privacy & fairness**, and **low-resource settings**.
    - The slides stay **text-light**; most detail is in the **speaker script**, not on the slide.

That’s the “at a glance” brief you can give them so they understand the number of slides, the storyline, and the overall vibe.
